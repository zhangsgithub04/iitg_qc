Quantum Computing Fundamentals

Quantum Gates Explorer

Quantum gates are the fundamental building blocks of quantum circuits. They transform qubits using reversible unitary operations, enabling superposition, entanglement, and interference.

ψ

What Quantum Gates Do

Quantum gates manipulate probability amplitudes by rotating state vectors. Instead of simply switching bits between 0 and 1, they transform qubits that can exist in superpositions.

U

Unitary Transformations

Quantum gates are represented as unitary matrices. This ensures that quantum operations preserve total probability and remain reversible.

U†U = I
∞

Why It Matters

By combining gates, quantum circuits can explore many possible states at once and use interference to amplify useful answers.

H

Hadamard Gate

Creates superposition by blending the |0⟩ and |1⟩ states.

H = 1/√2 [ 1 1 ]
      [ 1 -1 ]
X

Pauli-X Gate

Acts like a quantum NOT gate, flipping |0⟩ to |1⟩ and |1⟩ to |0⟩.

X = [ 0 1 ]
    [ 1 0 ]
S

Phase Gates

S and T gates introduce phase shifts, helping tune quantum interference patterns in algorithms.

S = [ 1 0 ]
    [ 0 i ]
⊕

CNOT Gate

The Controlled-NOT gate flips the target qubit only when the control qubit is in the |1⟩ state. It is one of the most important gates for creating entanglement.

T

Toffoli Gate

The Toffoli gate uses two control qubits and one target qubit. It shows that quantum circuits can reproduce classical digital logic.

⚭

Entanglement

Multi-qubit gates allow qubits to become correlated in ways that cannot be described independently, enabling powerful quantum behavior.
